# World Genesis — "The Riven Coast" (example run)

> A worked reference run. Generic low-fantasy setting chosen to show the framework without
> locking the toolkit to one genre.

## Identity
- **World name:** The Riven Coast
- **Genre:** low-fantasy
- **Era / tech level:** late-medieval (pike, early cannon, sail)
- **Scale of play:** regional power

## Physics & Rules Toggles
- **Magic:** low — rare hedge-magic and relics; no battlefield sorcery. Adds flavor, not a unit type.
- **Psionics / espers:** off
- **FTL / space travel:** off
- **Supernatural / divine intervention:** off
- **Other special rules:** winter quarter (every 4th turn) imposes attrition on deployed armies.

## Geography & Map
- **Map style:** a contested continental coast — three powers wedged between mountains and sea.
- **The player nation's region:** Haldmark, a hilly coastal march; one good harbor (Saltgate), a
  mountain border to the north, a river frontier (the Veil) to the east.
- **Notable features:** the Veil river (border + trade artery), Saltgate harbor (trade income),
  the Kelsdar passes (defensible northern chokepoints).

## Major Powers (the living world)
1. **Veska** — aggressive river-kingdom across the Veil; covets Saltgate. (rival sheet attached)
2. **The Adran League** — a mercantile city-league to the south; wealthy, militarily cautious.
3. **The Kelsdar Clans** — mountain confederacy to the north; raiders, courtable as buffer.

## Resource Set
- **grain** — feeds population & armies; shortfall → unrest/attrition. strategic? n
- **timber** — ships & fortifications; gates naval/fort projects. strategic? n
- **iron** — equipment tiers & artillery. strategic? **y**

## Tensions & Themes
- **Central tension:** Veska is rearming and wants the harbor; Haldmark must secure itself before
  the pressure becomes a war it can't win alone.
- **Tone seeds:** grounded, anxious, the weight of small-power survival between larger neighbors.
- **Starting clock:** Veska is expected to be war-ready by roughly Turn 6.

## Active Rules Pack
- **Turn cadence:** one turn = one season.
- **Special mechanics:** winter attrition (every 4th turn); iron shortage caps equipment upgrades.
