# World Genesis - "The Iron Retreat"

## Identity
- **World name:** The Iron Retreat
- **Genre:** sci-fi
- **Era / tech level:** information age
- **Scale of play:** city-state

## Physics & Rules Toggles
- **Magic:** off
- **Psionics / espers:** off
- **FTL / space travel:** off
- **Supernatural / divine intervention:** off
- **Other special rules:** no anomalous physics; infrastructure reliability and resource logistics dominate outcomes.

## Geography & Map
- **Map style:** Earth-like climate-fractured globe.
- **The player nation's region:** Arlin Dome sits on a former temperate inland corridor now pushed toward arid conditions, linked to drowned coastal trade by hardened convoy and relay routes.
- **Notable features:** flooded littoral salvage lanes, inland desal corridors, heat-risk migration funnels, and pipeline chokepoints.

## Major Powers (the living world)
1. **Helion Dome Compact** - protocol-first alliance of legacy domes; sees Arlin as useful but unproven and pressureable through standards.
2. **Brinewake Cartel** - flood-coast salvage and shipping syndicate; treats Arlin as both market and leverage point.
3. **Aquifer Directorate** - inland hydro-bureaucracy controlling deep water rights; views Arlin as a negotiator that can be debt-bound.
4. **Redline Security Front** - militarized drought-belt enclaves; sees Arlin as a buffer to harden or absorb.
5. **Continuity Assembly** - biotech governance bloc managing organ and fertility systems; treats Arlin as an unstable but important jurisdiction.

## Tensions & Themes
- **Central tension:** ecosystem collapse is approaching while city-states weaponize scarcity and standards to dominate each other.
- **Tone seeds:** gritty-political bargaining under survival pressure; every gain carries a social cost.
- **Starting clock:** terminal ecosphere collapse forecast in ~12 years (~48 turns).

## Active Rules Pack
- **Turn cadence:** one turn = one season.
- **Special mechanics for this setting:** prolonged deficits in pure_biomass or potable_water_rights increase unrest pressure; infrastructure stress amplifies event severity when stability is low.
